Wallpaper Removal All Types

Wallpaper Removal — clean walls, zero damage

DIY wallpaper removal routinely destroys drywall paper — leaving walls that require expensive skim-coating before painting. Our systematic, patient process leaves walls smooth, clean, and ready for a perfect result.

$1.50–$4.00
per sq ft

Standard vinyl-coated at the low end; fabric-backed, grasscloth, and multi-layer at the high end. Complete glue removal and minor wall repair included in all wallpaper jobs.

What's Included
  • Vinyl-coated & standard removal
  • Fabric-backed & grasscloth removal
  • Multi-layer & old paste types
  • Professional scoring (not razor)
  • Steam or chemical release agents
  • Section-by-section careful removal
  • Complete adhesive residue removal
  • Wall wash-down after removal
  • Minor wall repair included
  • Skim coat if needed (add-on)
  • Shellac-sealing of wall surfaces
  • Primed & ready to paint
Our Process
1
Test & IdentifyWe identify wallpaper type and test-peel in a hidden area to assess difficulty.
2
ScorePerforated scoring tool allows moisture to penetrate without damaging drywall.
3
Wet & RemoveSteam or chemical solution applied in sections. Patient, controlled removal.
4
Glue RemovalAll adhesive residue removed. Walls thoroughly washed down.
5
Repair & SealDamaged areas repaired, walls shellac-sealed, primed, ready for paint.
⚠️
Important: We strongly advise against painting over wallpaper. Seams telegraph through paint, edges lift, and moisture causes bubbling. Proper removal always looks better long-term.

Deck & Fence Staining Exterior Wood Care

Deck & Fence Staining — protected beauty through Portland winters

Outdoor wood exposed to Portland's rain and UV needs the right prep and the right product. We pressure-wash, brighten, sand, and apply premium penetrating stains by brush — not roller — for maximum wood penetration and years of lasting protection.

$1.50–$2.80
per sq ft

Typical 600 sqft deck: $900–$1,680. Includes pressure wash, wood brightener, sanding, and 1–2 coats of premium penetrating stain applied by brush.

What's Included
  • Commercial pressure washing
  • Wood brightener (oxalic acid) application
  • Sanding rough boards & splinters
  • Setting all nail heads & screws
  • Deck boards, rails & posts
  • Fences, gates & lattice
  • Pergolas & outdoor structures
  • Brush application — max penetration
  • Cabot, TWP, or Armstrong Clark stain
  • Semi-transparent or solid stain options
  • Board replacement identification
  • Full cure inspection before handover
Our Process
1
Pressure WashFull commercial wash strips dirt, mould, and grey oxidation from all surfaces.
2
Wood BrighteningOxalic acid brightener restores natural wood color and opens grain for stain absorption.
3
Sand & RepairRough boards sanded, nail heads set, damaged boards flagged.
4
Brush ApplicationPremium penetrating stain applied by brush, end-grain first. Maximum penetration.
5
Cure & InspectReturned after full cure to touch up any absorbed or missed areas.
🕐
Maintenance tip: A properly stained deck typically needs re-application every 2–4 years depending on sun and rain exposure. We offer annual inspection visits.
