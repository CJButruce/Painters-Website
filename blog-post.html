How to Make Your Exterior Paint Last 10+ Years

Marcus Donovan · November 12, 2024 · 7 min read

I've repainted hundreds of Portland homes that should never have needed repainting so soon. In almost every case, the failure wasn't the paint — it was the prep. Here's what actually separates a 5-year paint job from a 12-year one.

"Surface preparation is 80% of the job. The paint is almost secondary."

1. Pressure Wash Correctly (Most People Do This Wrong)

Before any prep work begins, the surface needs to be genuinely clean. Not surface-clean — clean down to the substrate. We use a 2,500–3,000 PSI commercial pressure washer with a 25-degree tip on siding and a 40-degree tip near trim and joints.

The key most homeowners miss: the wash direction. Always wash parallel to the seams of siding, never across them. Wash top to bottom. Let it dry fully — minimum 48 hours in summer, 72 in spring or fall.

2. Scrape Everything. Seriously, Everything.

New paint bonds to existing paint, which bonds to the substrate. If the existing paint is failing, your new paint inherits that failure. We spend hours hand-scraping every loose edge, feathering transitions, and wire-brushing anything that looks questionable.

  • Scraper angled at 30 degrees on siding
  • Wire brush on any rust or oxidized metal
  • Feather the scraped edges — don't leave hard ridges
  • Sand anything that still shows a visible edge after scraping

3. Prime Everything Bare, and Some Things That Aren't

Bare wood must be primed. Full stop. We use oil-based primer on bare wood because it penetrates deeper, blocks tannins, and outperforms latex primer at the substrate level.

4. Caulk Before You Paint (Not After)

Caulking is waterproofing. Every gap around window frames, door casings, trim-to-siding joints, and penetrations is a water highway into your wall. We caulk everything with a premium elastomeric siliconized acrylic — not the cheap stuff.

5. Use Premium Paint

There is a meaningful difference between Sherwin-Williams Duration and a contractor-grade budget product. Premium exterior paints have higher solids content, better UV inhibitors, and significantly better adhesion. The extra $30 per gallon pays for itself.

Our Recommended Exterior Lines

  • Sherwin-Williams Duration Exterior
  • Benjamin Moore Aura Exterior
  • Sherwin-Williams Emerald Exterior (for harsh exposures)

The Summary

Wash it properly. Scrape thoroughly. Spot-prime all bare wood. Caulk every joint. Apply two coats of premium paint with adequate dry time between. Do all five of these things and your exterior paint will last 10–12 years in the Pacific Northwest. Skip any of them and you're looking at 4–6.
